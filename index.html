What is a German sentence?
German basic sentence structure = Subject + Verb + Object Example: Ich trinke Tee. (I drink tea.)

Regular Verb Conjugation (-en verbs)

Person Ending Example (lernen)
ich-eich lerne
du-stdu lernst
er/sie/es-ter lernt
wir-enwir lernen
ihr-tihr lernt
sie/Sie-ensie lernen
Example with "lernen": ich lerne du lernst er lernt wir lernen Why is this important?
Because in German the verb MUST match the subject.
German Alphabet
Same as English (A–Z) + Ä, Ö, Ü, ß German is highly phonetic → You read what you see. Important Letters:
J = y (Ja) V = f (Vater) R = guttural sound ß = ss (Straße) CH Sound:
After e, i, ä, ö, ü → soft (ich) After a, o, u, au → hard (Bach) "sch" = sh
Schule → school Verb Root Rule:
Remove "-en" from infinitive lernen → lern → ich lerne Practice: Write 5 sentences using sch, ch, umlauts.

What are modal verbs?
They express ability, permission, desire, obligation. English: I can swim. I must study. German Structure:
Subject + Modal (position 2) + Object + Main Verb (END) Example: Ich kann Deutsch lernen. Wir müssen Pizza essen. Why does the main verb go at the end?
Because modal verbs push the second verb to final position.

A2 / B1 Complete Lessons (Fully Detailed Explanation)

This section prepares you for strong B1 level writing and speaking. Goal: Long sentences, correct word order, structured grammar.

Lesson 1 – Conjunctions (Sentence Connecting)

Why Conjunctions Matter
At A1 level, you speak in short sentences:
Ich lerne Deutsch. Es ist wichtig.

At B1 level, you connect them:
Ich lerne Deutsch, weil es wichtig ist.

That difference = exam level improvement.

1. Coordinating Conjunctions (No Word Order Change)
EnglishGermanVerb Position
andundNormal (Position 2)
oroderNormal
butaberNormal
Example:
Ich lerne Deutsch und ich arbeite.
Ich mag Tee, aber ich trinke Kaffee. Important: Verb stays in position 2.
2. Subordinate Conjunctions (Verb goes to END)
EnglishGermanRule
becauseweilVerb at end
althoughobwohlVerb at end
when/ifwennVerb at end
thatdassVerb at end
Structure:
Main clause + comma + conjunction + subject + object + VERB (at end) Example Breakdown:
Ich lerne Deutsch, weil es wichtig ist.
(ist goes to end) Common Mistake:
❌ weil es ist wichtig
✅ weil es wichtig ist

Lesson 2 – Indirect Speech (Indirekte Rede)

Purpose: Report what someone says without quoting directly. Direct:
Er sagt: "Ich komme."

Indirect:
Er sagt, dass er kommt.
Connector Table
Sentence TypeConnectorExample
StatementdassIch denke, dass es gut ist.
Yes/No QuestionobIch frage, ob er kommt.
W-questionW-word onlyEr fragt, wo du wohnst.
Formation Steps: 1. Identify type 2. Add correct connector 3. Move verb to end Example:
"Berlin ist schön." → Ich denke, dass Berlin schön ist. Common Exam Tip:
Use these starters: - Ich denke, dass… - Ich glaube, dass… - Ich bin der Meinung, dass…
